I currently work at Alpaca, a company I cofounded alongside William Buchwalter.

I am also a graduate student at the Montreal Institute for Learning Algorithms (Universite de Montreal) under the supervision of Laurent Charlin, where I am broadly interested in the empirical study of deep neural networks. In particular, I am interested in the dynamics of learning at scale, with a particular focus on large language models.

I have been extremely fortunate to have worked with number of excellent people in the past, including Jonathan Frankle at MosaicML, Benjamin Bratton and Nicolay Boyadjiev at The Terraforming, and a talented group of researchers at Microsoft Research Montreal.

Once upon a time, my summers were spent riding in helicopters, planting trees, and roaming around northern Canada.
