A Year In Books

I read a lot of good stuff this year and wanted to put together a short piece about it.

The contents of this piece encompass the things that I read between May 2019 and January 2020. As always, that which is read begets that which is thought; that which I consume, in turn consumes me.

A note on the methodology underpinning these essays: before I begin writing, I skim each book and search for my annotations - passages underlined, scribbles in margins, the occasional incredulous question mark or delighted exclamation. I copy these things down onto paper and as the compilation grows, a theme emerges. Dots are connected. There is an “aha” moment. I wonder if that’s why I like doing this - for the “aha”. I also wonder if the theme was there before I saw it, or if it is only there because I saw it. How long do you have to spend looking at a thing before you see a face in it?

I wonder if it matters.

Aha.

A fact: I have a ghoul. He follows me around in the day; he clutches at my heels and tangles himself in my hair. He sits under my desk at work and wanders around the houses of my friends. He perches on my pillow as I lay down to sleep, and as I close my eyes he crawls under my eyelids and into my ears and works his way up into the folds and crevasses of my frontal cortex, which is his favourite place on earth, and he asks, "What is real?

I don't know.

A disclaimer: Our ghoul in question is not a real and in-the-flesh ghoul, as you may have suspected, but a literary trope with origins in pre-Islamic Arabic religion, brought into vogue by William Beckford's Vathek in the late eighteenth century, and employed by me to give shape and form and sense of nauseating pervasiveness to a question that has plagued me for the better part of this past year. Because that's the thing with this question - it's this subtle haze, this thing that you can feel but cannot see, flickering around the edges of your periphery, so out of sight and out of reach that the best I can offer is a three word paraphrase, a weird metaphor about a ghoul, and a smattering of Things I Have Read.
